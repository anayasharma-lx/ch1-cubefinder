The gnomon
Each new square wraps an L-shaped gnomon around the previous one. The gnomon always has 2n−1 cells — the next odd number. Take differences of gnomons: you always get 2.

Why 2? The second differences of n² are always 2 = 2! — because n² is a degree-2 polynomial, and each level of differencing reduces the degree by 1. Two levels needed for a 2-dimensional shape.
