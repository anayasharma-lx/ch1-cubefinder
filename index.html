Why 2! and 3! ? For nk, the k-th level differences always equal k! This is because the leading term of nk is nk itself, and the k-th finite difference of nk equals k!. Geometrically: adding a gnomon to a square involves 1D and 2D growth, while adding a shell to a cube involves 1D, 2D, and 3D contributions — each level of geometry adding one more layer of differences.
