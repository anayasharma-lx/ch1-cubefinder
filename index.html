Cube Root Finder
To find ∛n: break n into prime factors, then sort them into three identical buckets. If you can fill three equal buckets, the product in one bucket is the cube root.
Try:
Step 1 — all prime factors of the number
↓ sort into 3 equal groups
Step 2 — fill three identical buckets (each bucket → one factor in the root)
Perfect squares — 1 level to a constant
nn²Diff L1Diff L2
Perfect cubes — 3 levels to a constant
nn³L1L2L3
Pattern: For n^k, you need exactly k levels of differences to reach a constant. Squares (k=2): level 2 differences = 2 = 2! Cubes (k=3): level 3 differences = 6 = 3! This means differences are a way to detect perfect powers — and to compute what that power is.
